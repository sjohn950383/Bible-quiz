2 Corinthians 3

1. 2Cor 3:1,2 - Paul asked if they needed letters of recommendation to the Corinthians or from them. He said that the Corinthians themselves were their letter, known and read by all, and written where?

  1. stone
  2. “our hearts”
  3. tablets
  4. "our memories"
2. 2Cor 3:3 - The Corinthians showed that they were a letter of Christ, prepared by Paul and Timothy, written not with ink but with what?

  1. goat's blood
  2. fire
  3. a cloud of witnesses
  4. the Spirit of the living God
3. 2Cor 3:4,5 - Paul had confidence through Christ toward God, but did not claim to be competent of himself to claim anything as coming from them. He said their competence came from whom?

  1. hard work
  2. much study
  3. his parents
  4. God
4. 2Cor 3:6 - Paul claimed that God had made them competent to be ministers of a new covenant, not of letter but of spirit; for the letter kills, but what gives life?

  1. the Spirit
  2. the blood of Christ
  3. the church
  4. the law
5. 2Cor 3:7 - The ministry of death, chiseled in letters on stone tablets, came in glory so that the people of Israel could not gaze at whose face?

  1. their own
  2. Aaron
  3. Moses
  4. Joshua
6. 2Cor 3:9 - For if there was glory in the ministry of condemnation, much more does the ministry of what abound in glory?

  1. riches
  2. good news
  3. justification
  4. glory
7. 2Cor 3:13 - What did Moses put over his face to keep the people of Israel from gazing at the end of the glory that was being set aside?

  1. veil
  2. sackcloth
  3. his robe
  4. panty hose
8. 2Cor 3:14,15 - Paul said that the Israelites' minds were hardened, and that to this very day, when they hear the old covenant read, the same veil is still there. Whenever Moses is read, what does he say the veil lies over?

  1. “their minds”
  2. the face of Moses
  3. the truth
  4. "their faces"
9. 2Cor 3:16,17 - But when one turns to the Lord, the veil is removed. The Lord is the Spirit, and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is what?

  1. law
  2. freedom
  3. punishment
  4. captivity
10. 2Cor 3:18 - Fill in the blanks: “And all of us, with unveiled faces, seeing the _____ of the Lord as though reflected in a mirror, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of _____ to another; for this comes from the Lord, who is the Spirit.”

  1. favor
  2. glory
  3. power
  4. zeal
