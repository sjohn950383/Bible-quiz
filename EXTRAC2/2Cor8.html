2 Corinthians 8

1. 2Cor 8:1,2 - Paul said God had granted grace to these churches and that during a severe ordeal of affliction, their abundant joy and extreme poverty overflowed in a wealth of generosity. Which churches was he talking about?

  1. Macedonian
  2. Ephesian
  3. Galatian
  4. Martian
2. 2Cor 8:3-5 - The Macedonian churches voluntarily gave according to their means, and even beyond their means. Entirely on their own, they begged us earnestly for the privilege of sharing in this ministry to the saints. And they did not do as was expected, but what did they give first to the Lord?

  1. firstborn
  2. firstfruits
  3. their tithes
  4. themselves
3. 2Cor 8:6,7 - Titus was urged that, as he had already made a beginning, so he should also complete this generous undertaking among the Corinthians. Paul said that they excelled in everything—in faith, in speech, in knowledge, in utmost eagerness, and in love. What was this "generous undertaking" that Paul wanted them to also excel in?

  1. sharing
  2. giving
  3. caring
  4. living
4. 2Cor 8:9 - For you know the generous act of our Lord Jesus Christ, that though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor, so that you might become rich through his what?

  1. power
  2. passion
  3. poverty
  4. providence
5. 2Cor 8:12 - For if the eagerness is there, according to what one has, not according to what one does not have, the gift is what?

  1. free
  2. returnable
  3. acceptable
  4. spent
6. 2Cor 8:15 - Fill in the blanks: "The one who ________ much did not have too much, and the one who ________ little did not have too little."

  1. gave
  2. had
  3. grew
  4. gathered
7. 2Cor 8:16,17 - Who did Paul thank God for, who put in his heart the same eagerness for the Corinthians that Paul had, and who was going to them of his own accord?

  1. Timothy
  2. Silas
  3. Barnabas
  4. Titus
8. 2Cor 8:18 - Paul sent along with Titus the brother who was famous among all the churches for his proclaiming the good news. He was chosen by the churches to carry what?

  1. this generous undertaking
  2. the communion
  3. the message
  4. the song books
9. 2Cor 8:20,21 - Paul said that they intended that no one should blame them in the way they administered the generous gift, for they intended to do what was right not only in the Lord's sight but also in the sight of others. He said they did this to avoid what?

  1. crowds
  2. blame
  3. criminals
  4. crying
10. 2Cor 8:22 - Paul said they were sending the brother who had often proved in many ways that he was zealous, and even more so because of his great confidence in the Corinthians. He said that Titus and the brothers were messengers of the churches and the glory of Christ. He asked the Corinthians to show those men, openly before whom, the proof of their love and the reason for his boasting about them?

  1. the Lord
  2. many nations
  3. the churches
  4. his mom
