1 Corinthians 11

1. 1Cor 11:1,2 - Paul told the Corinthians to be imitators of him, as he was of Christ. He commended them for remembering him in everything and for maintaining what just as he had handed them on to them?

  1. keepsakes
  2. memories
  3. parchments
  4. traditions
2. 1Cor 11:3 - Paul wanted the Corinthians to understand that the head of every man is who?

  1. Christ
  2. the elders
  3. his employer
  4. woman
3. 1Cor 11:3 - Paul wanted the Corinthians to understand that the head of his wife is who?

  1. Christ
  2. the elders
  3. her husband
  4. Dr. Phil
4. 1Cor 11:3 - Paul wanted the Corinthians to understand that the head of Christ is who?

  1. the angels
  2. God
  3. the Spirit
  4. Gabriel
5. 1Cor 11:4,5 - Paul said that any man who prays or prophesies with something on his head and any woman who prays or prophesies with her head unveiled disgraces their what?

  1. husband
  2. family
  3. hair
  4. head
6. 1Cor 11:6 - Paul said that if a woman will not veil herself, she should cut off her hair; and if it is disgraceful for a woman to have her hair cut off or to be shaved, she should do what?

  1. wear a veil
  2. stay at home
  3. cover her face
  4. run for cover
7. 1Cor 11:7 - Paul said, "For a man ought not to have his head veiled, since he is the image and reflection of God." Who did he say is the reflection of man?

  1. God
  2. Christ
  3. woman
  4. children
8. 1Cor 11:8-10 - Man was not made from woman, but woman from man. Neither was man created for the sake of woman, but woman for the sake of man. Paul said that the woman ought to have a symbol of authority on her head for this reason and because of who?

  1. the prophets
  2. the angels
  3. the elders
  4. the church
9. 1Cor 11:11,12 - In the Lord, woman is not independent of man or man independent of woman. For just as woman came from man, so man comes through woman. But what comes from God?

  1. food
  2. clothing
  3. shelter
  4. all things
10. 1Cor 11:14,15 - Paul asked, "Does not nature itself teach you that if a man wears long hair, it is degrading to him, but if a woman has long hair, it is her glory?" He said that her hair was given to her for a what?

  1. adornment
  2. blessing
  3. covering
  4. delight
11. 1Cor 11:17,18 - Paul said the Corinthians' meetings did more harm than good. He heard that when they came together as a church, there were what among them?

  1. strangers
  2. divisions
  3. demons
  4. wolves
12. 1Cor 11:19 - Paul said there have to be factions among them to show who among them are what?

  1. genuine
  2. baptized
  3. caring
  4. delivered
13. 1Cor 11:20,21 - Paul said that when they came together, it was not really to eat the Lord's supper. He said that when the time comes to eat, each went ahead with their own supper. One went hungry, while another became what?

  1. thirsty
  2. offended
  3. tired
  4. drunk
14. 1Cor 11:23,24 - Fill in the blank: "The Lord Jesus, on the night when he was betrayed, took a loaf of bread, and when he had given thanks, he broke it and said, 'This is my body that is for you. Do this in ___________ of me.'"

  1. obedience
  2. remembrance
  3. front
  4. honor
15. 1Cor 11:25,26 - Fill in the blank: "After supper Jesus took the cup, saying, 'This cup is the new covenant in my blood; do this, as often as you drink it, in remembrance of me.' For as often as you eat this bread and drink the cup, you proclaim the Lord's _____ until he comes."

  1. burial
  2. death
  3. creation
  4. obedience
16. 1Cor 11:27,28 - Whoever eats the bread or drinks the cup of the Lord in an unworthy manner will be answerable for the body and blood of the Lord. Before someone eats of the bread and drinks of the cup, they should do what to themselves?

  1. deny
  2. punish
  3. examine
  4. forgive
17. 1Cor 11:29 - For all who eat and drink without discerning the body, eat and drink what against themselves?

  1. judgment
  2. forgiveness
  3. punishment
  4. doubt
18. 1Cor 11:32 - Paul said that when we are judged by the Lord, we are disciplined so that we may not be what along with the world?

  1. ashamed
  2. buried
  3. condemned
  4. degraded
19. 1Cor 11:33,34 - Paul told his brothers and sisters that when they came together to eat, to wait for one another. If anyone was hungry, he said that they should eat at home, so that when they met together it might not result in judgment. He said that about the other things he would give what when he came?

  1. admonishments
  2. blessings
  3. criticisms
  4. instructions
